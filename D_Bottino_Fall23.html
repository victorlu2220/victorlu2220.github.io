Abstract

      Currently in the market, multiple technologies exist that help measure the physical properties of materials. However, these technologies can be either extremely expensive to purchase or too big in scale that makes them unsuitable for small-scale mechanical tests. The available microscale mechanical testing device could be hard to operate and require special training. One goal of this project is to create a user-friendly microdevice that can test the physical properties of a small biomaterial while, ideally, sustaining the cell life.
      The demand of settings for chemical testing varies from experiment to experiment, and therefore there isn’t a panacea chemical testing device and engineers need to carefully evaluate these demands and create devices that meet them. The second goal of this project is to create microdevices that meet the specific needs of each experiment to facilitate the research progress.

Accompolishments

      So far, multiple 3D models for chemical testing have been created, a circuit for a mechanical testing microdevice has been designed and tested and a linear actuator has been modeled.

      Figure I shows a chamber that is designed for cell-migration study. In the design, there is a vacancy at the bottom which allows the injection of hydrogel and nutrients that allows the migration and sustain cell viability on the device. Cells will grow on the meshes. The empty rectangular platforms are designated as observatory platforms that can fit well under the microscope.

      Figure II, III combined is a device that serves to do chemical testing. The device has 6 chambers, and they are all interconnected through the meshes on the platform walls(Figure II). The outer walls are designed to be inlets and outlets and will form a circulating system with the assistance of a pump. In Figure III, 2 long pipes can be seen at the bottom of the chamber; they are included so that drugs can be delivered to the system so study the dynamics. Figure IV shows an assembly of these structures, some margins are given to the meshes because after printing with Form 3 multiple times, the printer doesn’t print with good precision for small scale projects. Figure V shows a fluid stimulation of the system: a turbulent flow system exists on the platform while the drug delivery will be going through a relatively laminar at the bottom.

      The mechanical testing device is the most exciting part of my endeavour. Two force sensors, whose resistances change predictably with force applied to the surface, have been purchased. Based on the datasheet, a Force(g) - Resistance(ohms) relationship has been derived on Google Sheet; a relationship of higher accuracy needs to be obtained but it’s functionally reliable for experimental purposes. Using the principle of voltage divider, the resistance across the force sensor is calculated and further converted into force with a little bit code on Arduino. Along with the known surface property of the force sensor(8mm in diameter), the pressure on the surface can be calculated.

      An ultrasonic sensor will be incorporated into the system to measure the distance change such that Young’s Modulus can be calculated with the device. A pushing mechanism(shown in Figure XI) will be driven by a DC motor and controlled by a joystick to enable the movement of the device. A physical image of the current circuit is shown in Figure IX.
