A Universal Microchip for Cytocompatibility Study

The goal of this project is to build a cytocompatible platform for cell study. With this affordable and simple platform, researchers in the lab can introduce various innovative dental treatments to the microchip and evaluate their potential to promote cell growth in vitro.

A Deep Learning Based Cell Counter for Microscopy Image Analysis

Microscopy images are tricky to deal with sometimes. With stained cells in an image, it's not guaranteed that all stain will be apparent for human identification. Thus, a deep-learning based algorithm along with an unsupervised machine learning model are developed to help with such identification process.

Mussel inspired Bioadhesive

Mussels can cling to rocks for decades without being washed away by sea waves due to the secretion of byssal threads, with dopamine playing a significant role in enhancing the adhesive properties of these threads. In this study, we investigated whether dopamine can increase the adhesiveness of gelatin methacryloyl (GelMA), a well-known biocompatible hydrogel. Our goal is to develop a bioadhesive that could potentially replace surgical sutures in the future.

A Bioreactor for In Vitro Testing of Bone Graft

Unicameral Bone Cysts(UBCs) are found primarily in children and cause severe pain if left untreated. One available treatment is patient-specific bone implantation. In this project, I collaborated with my team to synthesize multiple artificial bone grafts that achieved Young's modulus comparable to that of native humerus bone. We also designed a functional bioreactor to study the in vitro behavior of the bone graft.
